General Admissions Careers Other

General

How much is tuition?
Tuition is $2,000. But if you get a job with one of our Hiring Partners after finishing the program, we'll refund $1,000 of your tuition (see Careers).

Is this an online program?
No. If you are accepted, you must be in Chicago for the duration of the program.

When does the next class start?
Our next class starts on April 21, 2014. If you are accepted, we will send you 60+ hours of prep work that must be completed before the first class session.

How is the program set up?
The program is split into two parts: class sessions and full-time immersion. Class sessions are mandatory and are held in the evenings on Mondays, Wednesdays, and Thursdays from 6:00 PM to 9:00 PM for 10 weeks.

During the day, you'll be working out of our space to do research, work on assignments and projects, and develop your portfolio — all in an immersive environment filled with your peers who are doing the same.

We also host guest speakers and sponsored workshops on weeknights, and lab sessions on most Saturdays.

What will a typical day be like?
We find the best learning happens in a self-directed, discovery-oriented environment. So while you’ll learn from instructors twice a week in the evening, the way you spend your days is up to you.

How is my work evaluated?
There are no grades, exams or certificates. When you join DESIGNATION, it’s because you want to begin a career as a professional designer. We encourage you to act accordingly.

However, to help you keep track of your progress, you will periodically be asked to create portfolio deliverables and technical posts detailing your design process and thinking.

What if I don’t like or am not interested in one of the topics?
Digital design is a large field, and our curriculum covers many topics. We understand that not everyone will become a master UX practitioner, graphic designer, and front-end engineer all at once. Our goal is to expose you to the fundamental topics in modern-day digital design, and let you choose your own path to becoming a great designer.

What's the time commitment?
When you are accepted into the program, you will be expected to complete 60+ hours of prep work before the first class session. During the program, you will need to dedicate at least 60 hours a week to the program, for assignments, projects, and portfolio work.

Can’t I just learn design from the internet or by reading books?
You certainly can. In fact, we utilize several textbooks and online resources, including learning tools and relevant articles, to supplement our immersive program. At DESIGNATION, your progress is guided by a team of passionate and talented working professionals. You'll also be working alongside and collaborating with your peers in a completely immersive environment, day in and day out.

It's an experience you can't get online.

What will I need to bring for the program?
You will need to bring your own laptop (PC or Mac, but we strongly recommend a MacBook Pro with Retina display), with Adobe Photoshop and Adobe Illustrator already installed.* We'll provide you with everything else you need for the program, including tools, textbooks, and more.

*If you don't already have Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator, we recommend the Adobe Creative Cloud, which gives you access to these two software programs.

What can I expect to get out of the program?
As any professional designer can tell you, good design is just as much about the why as the how. We’ll teach you what it means to create more than just pretty layouts, but fully realized user experiences. You will be assigned projects and assignments to work on during the course’s duration. When it’s over, if you’ve put in the hours and dedication we expect, you’ll walk away with a killer portfolio, and some hot leads on a new job.

What if I already know some of the topics you teach?
Chances are, if you’re reading this page and are interested in DESIGNATION, you already have some past experience or working knowledge in design. Great! But we cover a vast array of topics, and the program is designed to make you well-rounded and agile enough to integrate seamlessly into any part of the design process.

If a class covers a topic you already know inside and out, you are encouraged to step outside to continue working on your portfolio.

Admissions

What kind of students do you look for?
We look for highly motivated individuals who demonstrate maturity, persistency in problem-solving, and show a genuine interest in design. Top candidates will have some technical experience, either in design or programming. All applicants should be able to commit more than 600 hours over 10 weeks to the program.

We do not discriminate based on race, color, sex, religion, national origin, age, disability, or orientation. The male-to-female ratio of past cohorts has been about 40% male and 60% female.

What's the application process look like?
The first step is to apply. Once we've reviewed your application and our team deems you a candidate that is ready for the program, the next step is a 20-minute Skype interview, an extended dialogue based on the answers in your application.

If you're in Chicago, we'd love to meet you in person.

What happens once I am accepted?
If accepted, you must notify us of your decision to enroll within one week, along with a $600 deposit to reserve your seat in the program. Once you are confirmed, we'll send you 60+ hours of curated prep work that you must complete before the program begins.

The remaining tuition balance must be paid in full one week before the first class session.

Do you accept foreign candidates??
Yes. Tuition for students from outside the US is $4,000, but if you accept a job with one of our Hiring Partners, you will get a refund of $3,000. This is due to the added difficulties and costs associated with the visa sponsorship process needed from a Hiring Partner.

Careers

How can you help me get a job as a designer?
We've teamed up with a select group of innovative and tech-focused Hiring Partners who are looking to hire design talent. These companies are thrilled to meet new graduates, and are excited about the opportunity of having you join their team.

Do you guarantee a job?
Nothing is guaranteed, but our #1 and only goal is to turn you into a hireable candidate. As long as you put in the necessary time and effort into the program, you will become an employable designer. The second half of the program focuses on resources and strategies for entering the job market so that when you graduate you’ll be prepared to discuss job opportunities with our Hiring Partners.

The last day of the program is Portfolio Night, a unique event where you can meet the companies who are looking for candidates, and showcase all your hard work from the past ten weeks.

If you end up working with one of our Hiring Partners, we'll refund $1,000 of your tuition.

What's the average salary for a designer?
The design field is wide-ranging, and your salary depends on which field you go into. For example, a UX designer's median salary is $80,000, while a visual designer could make anywhere from $60,000 to $100,000. During the program, you'll learn more about yourself and what type of design you like best, and we'll help you pursue whichever path you choose to take.

How long will it take for me to get a job?
Depending on your portfolio, you should expect anywhere from 1 week to 3 months, after graduation.

Other

What is your refund policy?
The $600 deposit is nonrefundable. The remaining tuition amount is refundable if you drop within 24 hours after the first class session.

Will I receive a certificate upon completion of the program?
We do not give certificates upon completion of the program. But that’s OK, because in the design world, your work should speak for itself. If you're looking for a job, our Hiring Partners care more about your portfolio than any piece of paper.

Why should I learn design?
Whether you want to find a new job, integrate design-driven principles into your company, or create a whole new startup, design is an integral part of any business. Read what others are saying about the design revolution: Fast Company, The Next Web, PandoDaily, Medium, VentureBeat.

What makes you different from formal design schools or universities?
We don’t like to think of ourselves as a school, per se. More of a training ground. We’re teaching you how to do a very specific thing, and giving you a very particular set of skills so you can start a successful design career. Everything we do, and everything we teach, has that goal in mind.

We think it’s a failing of traditional education that it can cost hundreds of thousands of dollars, only to come out the other end with no hireable skills or abilities.

DESIGNATION is a fraction of the cost of higher education, and we don't leave you hanging upon graduation — we'll help you find a job as a designer (see Careers). Plus, we actually include the cost of textbooks in your tuition.

How do you make money?
We've partnered with a select group of technology companies that seek designers. Our Hiring Partners have first access to interviewing our new graduates. If they find that you're a good match for them, they pay us a placement fee based on your first year salary. This allows us to:
  • help our job-seeking students find full-time employment and lower their tuition costs
  • help companies find design talent without the recruiting hassles
  • have the resources to continue to improve the quality of the program

Who can I contact if I have questions?
You can send us an email at team@designation.io.
